What I Do

I investigate questions at the intersection of insurance design, provider regulation, and geographic access to care. My work draws on large-scale administrative claims data, causal inference methods like difference-in-differences and synthetic controls, and GIS-based spatial analysis to produce research that is both methodologically rigorous and practically relevant to payers, policymakers, and health systems.

Background

Before pursuing my PhD in Health Systems & Services at the University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences, I spent five years at Blue Cross Blue Shield of Arkansas in roles spanning product modernization, data strategy, and senior data analysis. That experience shapes how I approach research — I ask questions that matter to the people making coverage and policy decisions, and I present answers in terms they can act on.

I'm also a published researcher, with work in PLOS One and Frontiers in Public Health on geospatial health analytics, and I've presented at the American Society of Health Economists (ASHEcon) national conference.
